## What surprised or challenged assumptions

**The clearest, most explicit statement yet that the gap is behavior, not awareness.** Where Noor's "aware but doesn't sustain change" pattern had to be inferred from her behavior, and Dooney's had to be inferred from a self-confidence/behavior mismatch, Emily states it directly: she knows where her money goes, and her core flaw is "no urge to budget, not lack of awareness." This is the strongest single data point yet for the Filter B / behavior-change-after-awareness reading — and, notably, it is not a self-image/behavior mismatch the way Noor's and Dooney's are. Emily's 6/10 self-rating is consistent with what she describes (moderate awareness, admitted impulsiveness), not contradicted by it. That makes her a different kind of data point than Noor or Dooney: not "says she's careful, isn't" — just "knows, and doesn't act."

**A price point that sits between the two existing numbers, attached to a concept that also sits between the two existing concepts.** $10/month, for a concept with light setup and some prescriptive suggestions (flagging overspending, margin adjustments) but not Dooney's full dashboard. Read next to Tamara ($5–8, zero setup, zero suggestions) and Dooney ($15–20, pie charts, cut-back suggestions), there is now a rough gradient: price seems to rise with feature richness and setup. This is still three different people reacting to three different concepts, not a controlled comparison — but it is the first time the pattern has looked directionally consistent rather than just scattered.

**The strongest purchase-intent signal in the project so far.** "Would sign up today if available" is a stronger stated commitment than anything from Tamara or Dooney, both of whom reacted positively but stopped short of saying they'd act immediately.

**A design input not previously named: forward-looking nudges, not just retrospective summaries.** Every earlier concept (Tamara's, Dooney's, and the "Money in Plain English" core positioning in `brand-guide.md`) is structured around a weekly look-back. Emily explicitly wants account-level warnings for the next few days — a proactive, real-time nudge layer ("hey girl, slow down"), not only a retrospective plain-language summary. This is a new product-shape question the venture hasn't had to consider yet.

**A second counter-signal on the TikTok/Instagram channel bet — from a different platform.** Emily is Twitter-primary and follows no personal finance creators, same as Dooney (Instagram-primary, deleted TikTok, no creators followed) — two different platforms, same absence of dedicated finance-creator engagement. She does recognize Shark Tank figures for money quotes, which hints that mainstream business-media personalities (not niche #FinTok creators) may have more reach with at least some of this demographic — worth holding as a loose lead, not a channel decision.

**Life-stage fit is cleanly established, unlike Dooney's.** "First big girl job with meaningful income" is the same trigger marker used to qualify Noor and Tamara as target customers. Unlike Dooney, Emily's interview does establish this directly, so she is treated as a third confirmed target-customer interview, not a candidate.
