# Brand Guide — Money in Plain English

---

## 1. Colors

**Primary — Sage Green**
`#5A9171`
Used for: logo, headings, buttons, key UI elements.
The main brand color. Natural and grounded, not loud. Reads as "money" without the aggression of bright green.

**Secondary — Soft Sage**
`#87B89D`
Used for: subheadings, captions, supporting text, tints.
A lighter version of the primary. Creates tonal range without introducing a second hue.

**Supporting neutrals**
- Body text: `#2D2D2D` (near-black — easier on the eye than pure black)
- Light background tint: `#F2F7F4` (barely-there sage — works for cards, section backgrounds)
- White: `#FFFFFF`

---

## 2. Fonts

Both fonts are available in Google Slides and Microsoft PowerPoint.

**Headlines: Nunito Bold**
Rounded, modern, warm. Feels approachable without being playful. Pairs well with a brand that wants to feel human, not clinical.

**Body / Supporting text: Lato Regular**
Clean and highly readable at any size. Widely available across all platforms. Use Lato Light for captions or secondary labels.

**Pairing in practice:**
- Slide title or page heading → Nunito Bold, `#5A9171` or `#2D2D2D`
- Body paragraph → Lato Regular, `#2D2D2D`
- Caption or label → Lato Light, `#87B89D`

---

## 3. Brand Voice

**Clear. Calm. Human.**

**Clear** — No jargon, no financial-industry language, no acronyms. If someone who has never thought about budgeting can read it and immediately understand it, it's right. If it sounds like a bank wrote it, rewrite it.

**Calm** — The target customer is already overwhelmed. The sheer number of things they're managing simultaneously (loans, rent, credit cards, everyday expenses) is what makes money feel hard. Every word we write should lower the temperature, not raise it. No urgency, no alarm, no scare tactics.

**Human** — We are talking to a person who already knows they should do better and feels bad that they haven't. We don't lecture. We don't celebrate wins performatively. We just show up consistently and tell them what's actually going on, without judgment.

## 4. Tagline

**Your money, finally in plain english.**

---

## 5. Company Description

Money in Plain English is for people who are newly managing their own finances and don't quite have it figured out yet. We connect to your bank account and automatically turn your transactions into a plain-language picture of where your money actually went — no spreadsheet, no budget to build, no categories to maintain. Once a week, you get a short summary in plain english: what came in, what went out, and what that means. Most financial apps assume you're already ready to manage your money. We start one step before that. We help you actually see what's happening before we ask you to change anything.
